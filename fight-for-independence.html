Fight for independence



Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, born on October 31, 1875, in Nadiad, Gujarat 🌍, was one of the foremost leaders in India’s struggle for independence 🏛️. A trained barrister ⚖️, Patel initially returned to India to pursue his career but soon became deeply involved in the freedom movement after being inspired by Mahatma Gandhi’s philosophy of non-violence and civil disobedience ✊.

A successful lawyer by profession, his life encountered a turning point when Mahatma Gandhi chose him as his deputy commander to lead the Kheda Satyagraha in 1918. Thus, as the leader of a peasants’ protest, Vallabhbhai Patel found the trajectory of his life turning towards a path of public service.In 1924, Patel was elected President of the Ahmedabad Municipal Board. Taking charge, he revamped the drainage, sanitation, cleanliness and water distribution systems of Ahmedabad.To their sheer awe, the citizens witnessed the President of the Board taking up a broom and dust cart himself, and cleaning the Harijan quarter of the city. In him, the city of Ahmedabad found a new Hero. Vallabhbhai Patel became increasingly involved with the struggle for independence. It was his role in the Bardoli Satyagraha of 1928 that elevated him to a new pinnacle of national glory. The peasant movement that became a subject of great discussion across the nation demonstrated the Sardar’s organisational capacity and enthusiasm for tireless action. It was here that he earned the title of ‘Sardar’, the fond epithet by which he continues to be remembered and revered. Sardar Patel went on to become one of the foremost pillars of the national struggle for freedom.

In 1931, he was elected President of the Indian Nation Congress at its Karachi Session. At a time when the nation was in tumult over the execution of Bhagat Singh, Sukhdev and Rajguru, he made a sombre speech that reflected the sentiment of the hour. With the passing of the Indian Independence Act 1947, the long-cherished dream of freedom was finally at the doorstep. However, massive obstacles lay ahead. At the time of Independence, India consisted of British India and the Princely States. There were 17 BritishIndian provinces, and the Princely States- comprising about two fifths of the geographic territory of the country- numbered more than 560. While the Indian Independence Act ceded control of British India to the Indian Government, rulers of the Princely States were given the option to decide whether they wanted to accede to India or Pakistan or neither. His first major involvement came in 1917, when he led the Kheda Satyagraha, advocating for the reduction of land revenue taxes for farmers suffering from a famine 🌾. Patel’s leadership proved instrumental in negotiating a settlement with the British government 🇬🇧, earning him the title of “Sardar,” meaning leader 👑. This marked the beginning of his long and impactful association with the independence movement. In 1928, Patel further demonstrated his leadership during the Bardoli Satyagraha, where farmers were protesting against land revenue hikes 📉. Patel’s successful organization of the peasants led to the government’s decision to return their lands 🏡, further solidifying his reputation as a tenacious and effective leader.

Patel's unwavering commitment to the freedom struggle saw him take part in major movements such as the Civil Disobedience Movement in 1930 🚶‍♂️, where he was arrested for protesting the British salt laws 🧂. Despite facing multiple imprisonments, Patel remained a staunch advocate of non-violence 🕊️ and stood by Gandhi’s leadership. His resolute nature was evident during the Quit India Movement in 1942 ✋, where he played a key role in mobilizing grassroots support for the demand of an immediate end to British rule. Though Patel was initially hesitant about the idea of partition ✂️, his pragmatic approach led him to support the inevitable division of the country in 1947 🇮🇳, believing it was the only way to avoid further violence. He understood the importance of maintaining unity within the newly independent nation 🌏, and his efforts were crucial in the integration of more than 500 princely states into the Indian Union, ensuring that they aligned with the Indian government 🏛️.

As the Home Minister in the first Cabinet of independent India 💼, Patel’s leadership was pivotal during these crucial times, ensuring that India remained united despite the turmoil following independence 🔗. His strategic mind, combined with a deep sense of responsibility and commitment to the people of India, made him one of the key figures in the Indian independence movement ✊, laying the foundations for a unified and prosperous nation 🌟.
