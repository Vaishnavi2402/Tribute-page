Early Life

Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel was born on 31st October 1875 in the small town of Nadiad in Gujarat, India 🌍.Vallabhbhai Patel was born on 31st October 1875 in Nadiad, Gujarat. A successful lawyer by profession, his life encountered a turning point when Mahatma Gandhi chose him as his deputy commander to lead the Kheda Satyagraha in 1918. Thus, as the leader of a peasants’ protest, Vallabhbhai Patel found the trajectory of his life turning towards a path of public service. He belonged to a modest Patidar family, known for its agricultural background 🌾. His father, Jhaverbhai Patel, was a farmer and had served in the army of Rani Lakshmibai of Jhansi 🏰, while his mother, Ladbai, was a deeply religious woman who instilled strong values in her children 🙏. Vallabhbhai displayed remarkable determination and a practical mindset from a young age 💪. Despite limited resources, Patel pursued his education diligently 📚. He initially studied in local schools and later completed his matriculation at the age of 22 🎓, which was considered late by conventional standards ⏳. However, his delayed academic journey never hindered his ambitions 🚀.

There had been an instance when Sardar Patel treated a painful boil 😖 without any hesitation using a hot iron rod 🔥🔧. At the age of 22️⃣, when everyone completes their graduation 🎓, he completed his matriculation 📜, and due to this, everyone thought he would be doing ordinary jobs 👨‍💼. After completing his matriculation, Sardar Patel continued his studies 📖 and became a law graduate ⚖️. Later, he traveled to England 🇬🇧 to become a barrister 👨‍⚖️. After returning to India 🇮🇳, he continued practicing law in Ahmedabad, Gujarat ⚖️🏛️.

Vallabhbhai aspired to become a barrister ⚖️, and after working hard to save money 💼, he traveled to England to study law at the Middle Temple Inn in 1910 ✈️. In just over two years, he completed his course with distinction and returned to India as a successful barrister 👨‍⚖️. His early life showcased his resilience, dedication, and strong work ethic 💯, which later defined his leadership and legacy as the "Iron Man of India" 🛠️🇮🇳.
