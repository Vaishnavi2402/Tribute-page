Born on October 31, 1875, in Nadiad, Gujarat, Vallabhbhai Patel belonged to a humble farming family. Despite limited resources, he pursued his education with determination. When the British government imposed heavy taxes on farmers in Gujarat, Patel led a peaceful protest, urging farmers to refuse payment. The movement was successful, forcing the British to roll back the taxes. This victory earned him the title "Sardar," meaning leader. After India gained independence in 1947, Patel was appointed as the first Deputy Prime Minister and Home Minister. His most remarkable achievement was the integration of over 560 princely states into the Indian Union. At the time of independence, these princely states had the option to join India, Pakistan, or remain independent. Patel, with his strategic diplomacy, persuasion, and, when required, the use of force, convinced almost all of them to become part of India. The most challenging states to integrate were Hyderabad, Junagadh, and Jammu & Kashmir. Patel successfully brought Junagadh and Hyderabad under Indian control without major conflict. His efforts ensured that India remained united, preventing fragmentation. His role in this integration earned him immense respect and solidified his legacy as the unifier of India. Apart from unification, Patel was instrumental in setting up India's modern administrative framework.
